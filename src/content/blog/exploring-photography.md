In this new exploration of life without social media, _not that I think that's fully pertinent right now_, I have started investing more time into photography. It's primarily in the
interest of improving my watercolor and other paint + draw aspects I'm trying to improve.

I have two magazines I intended to read and learn some basics from:
- Complete Photography Masterclass By Digital Photographer
- Canon Photographer's Handbook

Right now I am working with the [EOS R50 V](https://www.usa.canon.com/shop/p/eos-r50-v-body) with the [RF-S14-30mm F4-6.3](https://www.usa.canon.com/shop/p/rf-s14-30mm-f4-6-3-is-stm-pz) that it came with and a [RF-S18-150mm F3.5-6.3](https://www.usa.canon.com/shop/p/rf-s18-150mm-f3-5-6-3-is-stm). They're considered amateur lenses
to my knowledge and what I've read so far. I have purchased a [RF45mm F1.2 STM](https://www.usa.canon.com/shop/p/rf45mm-f1-2-stm) As a "lower" cost, higher quality tester for something like food photography. I wanted this one in particular becuse of the F1.2 quality where my other two lenses are F4-6.3 and f3.5-6.3. With these three
as my starting lenses I want to start getting more into being able to study things visually and get a better grip on composistion, perspectives, angles and so on.

The Masterclass managzine has so far done a great job of explaining and giving concrete examples of the what and how, so I feel confident. I suppose my next dilemma is
where I can take photography without it invading privacy or being against the rules. I did go to a Hawks game some months ago with my camera and learned that you can't bring
certain cameras. So, I won't be trying sports games anymore, though it would be nice to take some pictures of the Atlanta Dream.

Anyways, I have been going and testing my camera at my community garden and doing close up/focus shots of my marigolds (and hopefully soon my tomatoes). Had it not been for the back porch now having this awning and netting, I could probably get some interesting shots of my backyard birds at the feeder.

At some point I hope to be able to share those photos here and keep track of my leanring process as I go. Maybe I could dedicate a tab to just the photos that I like in the sea of ones I may take. I think it will be a nice addition.
