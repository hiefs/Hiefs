Finished Chants of Sennaar this morning, marking this my first game review post of this year. This is technically the second game I've completed after **Is This Seat Taken**, which was a great game. 

Anyways, I completed Chants of Sennaar with a total of 8.6 hrs, unlocking 84% of the achievements (21/25).

It was very enjoyable. The visuals are fantastic, they can be bright, vivid, but also dark and errie in some places. The language puzzle parts were some of the most enjoyable. The game does a wonderful job creating ambiance in that it was able to push me to feel for these characters, their situation, all while working the muscles in my brain to solve some of the puzzles. 

There were points where I got lost. And I will admit there were at least two sectiosn in which I looked up wher emy next step was supposed to be, howev er I did not look up anything that would point me to solving what the words are in the language. I did all that stuff raw. 

I don't fault the game for that I just wasn't being torough, and the story overall was very engaging especially at the end. I won't be giving games ratings because I tend to lean toward more of a _"If I had a good time it's a good game"_ method of going about things that giving it something like X/10 feels silly for someone as myself who isn't a game journalist or anything beyond your common consumer. i'll leave that to the pros. Instead I'll simply do a Recommend/Don't Recommend system that feels more faithful to my place int he gaming ecosystem.

I **Highly Recommend** Chants of Sennaar for any person that loves a well made yet simple game and puzzles of course.
